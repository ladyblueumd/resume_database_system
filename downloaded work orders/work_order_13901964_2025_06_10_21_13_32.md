I arrived at 930pm. The GC requested for me to arrive at 9pm. This was too short of notice and I arrived at the time I could.

The Gc and the electrician were onsite and the Team Lead for the evening Samantha
arranged for receiving to move any assets that were blocking the pallets.

We went into the loading bay and the Elo machines were in receiving and not secured.
I took the Elo displays and went to the Lower Level and the GC and the electrician brought the MACs to the Lower Level.
We located and discussed the placement of each MAC, then I verified that the inventory was complete and the install could take place.

The GC informed me that all of the installs were freestanding.
The floor plans had each location and none were on an end cap.
We decided that we would work together on each MAC and get the first one up and running.


The Team lead and all of the employees left for the evening after we removed all of our inventory from the second floor. Once they left we did not have access to the second floor.
They left a self checkout open for food and drink, but left us locked in the store.

The GC did not have the anchor bolts for the installation.
He informed me that he used the old anchor bolts the night before and assumed that it would be the same process.
We tried using different bolts for the floor plate but the GC did not have the correct sized drill bit to save the installation.

The GC did not let us know that he did not have any anchor bolts until after the employees left and we were locked in the store for the evening.

So after trying to secure the MAC to the floor and failing, we started doing a site survey to see what else was not ready for the install.
There were no lan drops at the locations where the new MACs would be installed

We accessed that no existing lan drops could reach the new location for one MAC install and no drops were in place nor nearby to supply data.
There were no PoE injectors for the new MACs as well. There are 5 that need to be installed and the current MACs have PoE injectors in the top cabinet.

We had to wait until the next Team Lead arrived at 4am.
We had to wait to wrap the MAC units in cardboard to prevent damage until the reschedule.
All the cardboard was on the second floor, which was alarmed and inaccessible.
The electrician left at 4am and the GC wrapped the MACs in cardboard and I re-boxed the data poles and arranged receiving to accommodate the MACs while preserving a walkway between the dock and where they would be stored.

I took what pictures I could to document, but this will be a reschedule after the lan drops are completed and the next GC and obtain the correct anchor bolts.
