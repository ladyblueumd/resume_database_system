Service Description

YOU MUST PRE-CERTIFY FOR THIS WORK ORDER THROUGH OUR OPEN RECRUITMENT PRIOR TO ASSIGNMENT




SCOPE OF WORK

The purpose of this assignment is to perform routine Preventative Maintenance (PM) in the form of a general cleaning of Bank Note Recyclers (BNR) and Bulk Coin Recyclers (BCR) found in retail stores. These jobs are simple with easy-to-follow video instructions and don’t require any existing skills or knowledge of the recyclers (this is not breakfix).

Technicians are required to watch a prerequisite video (under 15 minutes) to become “pre-certified” as part of the recruitment process. After pre-certification, your first work order will require on-the-job training (OJT) in the form of remote support. After you successfully complete your first assignment you will be able to pick up as many jobs as you can accommodate in your schedule. 

You can access the recruitment here: https://app.fieldnation.com/recruitments/486 

Reference Guides and Instruction Manuals can be found here: ********************************




PHYSICAL REQUIREMENTS

The ability to lift a ‘bucket’ of coins up to 30-50 lbs, the ability to bend down and reach, and the ability to maneuver in tight spaces.




FEES

Our base fee is $80 for the first two hours. Outlier and remote locations are posted at a rate of $110 or $195 for the first two hours to cover travel costs. Additional hour(s) on site are paid at a rate of $40 per hour regardless of location.




SERVICE HOURS

Service schedules are pre-arranged between the kiosk manufacturer and the retail location. We cannot accept any counter-offers for adjustments to the service date or arrival time due to having no flexibility with the appointment.

Work order schedules are created with a 3-hr arrival window. You must arrive on site and begin work during the arrival window.

You must contact KSG immediately if you are running late or cannot make it to your same-day assignment. Technicians who repeatedly arrive late or cancel work orders with less than a 24 hr notice will be blocked from future assignments.




REMOTE SUPPORT

The fastest and most effective way to contact us is through Live Chat. You can start a conversation following this link, or by visiting our instruction manual website:

https://direct.lc.chat/17277198/

Kiosk Services Group Support can be reached at 720-370-2760. Kiosk Services Group's hours of operation are Mon-Fri 8am-5pm MST. Our Preventative Maintenance support team is available from 6am to 10pm EST. PM Support can be reached through live chat or by calling 720-370-2760 ext. 2.




REQUIRED TOOLS

You are required to bring the following tools and materials with you to every work order.

Foam swabs
Microfiber cloths
Spray bottle with water
Plastic scraper tool
Screwdrivers: Phillips (#0, #1), Flathead, Torx (T20)
Needle nose pliers or a small wrench

The following tools are highly recommended to bring:

Shop Vac with blower (or electric duster)
N95 Dust Mask




DRESS CODE

Professionalism is of the utmost importance so technicians have a required dress code including a collared shirt, long pants, and closed-toe shoes. 

You will get dusty! We strongly recommend wearing a dark (navy blue/black) polo shirt or button-down work shirt (such as Carhartt, Dickies). Jeans are OK, please no rips or tears.




PREP

Review the Reference Guide for very valuable information. This includes preparation, helpful tips and tricks, and other FAQs.
Review the Instruction Manual for the machine you will be working on. All machines are accessible on the website, but to make this especially easy, we've provided the direct link to the kiosk you will be servicing in the buyer custom fields.
Set an ETA. This work order is a hard start. You must arrive within the scheduled window. If you are running late, please contact Kiosk Services Group immediately.
Print the LOA that is attached to this work order to bring with you.

ONSITE

Arrive on site on time and in the approved dress code.
Check-in
Locate the store manager and inform them that you are here to perform Preventative Maintenance of their Revolution kiosk.
Take a photo of the machine's screen to capture the date/time and Kiosk ID.
Follow the on-screen videos to perform all cleaning tasks. Refer back to the Reference Guide and Instruction Manual as needed.
If you run into any issues on site, contact KSG.

CLOSE OUT

Enter detailed closing notes confirming that the task is complete. If you experienced any delays on site please make sure that this is included in your closing notes.
Make sure your time spent on site is accurate.
Check out when leaving the kiosk.




ESTIMATED TIME ON SITE

Warning: Time on site and GPS coordinates will be reviewed for accuracy.
